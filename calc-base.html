2.5 mm² Cables in a 1" Conduit

  1. Home
  2. Projects

To calculate how many 2.5 mm² cables can fit in a 1" conduit, we will consider the following data:

  1. Outer diameter of a 2.5 mm² cable: Approximately 3.4 mm (as per the table provided earlier).
  2. Inner diameter of a 1" conduit: Approximately 25.4 mm (or 2.54 cm).

Calculation:

  1. Area of the conduit:

    Aconduit = π × (25.4 mm / 2)2 ≈ 506.7 mm2

  2. Area of a 2.5 mm² cable:

    Acable = π × (3.4 mm / 2)2 ≈ 9.0 mm2

  3. Calculation of the number of cables:

    Using 40% maximum occupancy: Ausable = Aconduit × 0.4 ≈ 202.7 mm2

    Ncables = Ausable / Acable ≈ 202.7 / 9.0 ≈ 22.5

Rounding down, approximately 22 cables of 2.5 mm² can fit in a 1" conduit, considering a maximum occupancy of 40%.
