Explanation of the Formulas in the Cable Tray Suggestion Code

1. Cable Area

The first formula is used to calculate the area occupied by the cables:

const cableArea = Math.PI * Math.pow(cableValue / 2, 2) * quantityValue;

Explanation:

  • cableValue: Diameter of the cable.
  • cableValue / 2: Radius of the cable.
  • Math.pow(cableValue / 2, 2): Area of the cable's cross-section (circle).
  • Multiplied by quantityValue (number of cables) to obtain the total area occupied by the cables.

2. Allowed Area

The allowed area for the cable tray is calculated as follows:

const allowedArea = cableArea / 0.4;

Explanation:

  • The total area occupied by the cables is divided by the allowed occupancy rate (40%).
  • This means the cable tray must be at least 2.5 times the area occupied by the cables.

3. Cable Tray Area

Within the loop that looks for the suitable cable tray, the area is calculated like this:

const trayArea = tray.height * tray.width;

Explanation:

  • The area of the cable tray is calculated by multiplying its height by its width.
  • It checks if this area is greater than or equal to the allowedArea.

Summary

The code determines the total area occupied by the cables, considering their diameter and quantity, calculates the minimum required area of the cable tray to maintain a safe occupancy rate, and iterates through a list of possible dimensions for the cable trays, suggesting the smallest one that meets the area criteria.
